Great Falls

About Great Falls

Great Falls, Montana is a city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences. The city is located in the central part of Montana and is often referred to as Montana's basecamp. It is situated between four mountain ranges, three rivers, two wilderness areas, and one wildlife refuge, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Missouri River passes through the city, and there are 53 miles of picturesque trail along the river that runs through town.


Great Falls is a city that has something for everyone. For art lovers, the city boasts over 25 wall murals that make downtown Montana's biggest art gallery. The city is also home to ten museums, including the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center near Giant Springs and the C. M. Russell Museum and Original Log Cabin Studio on the city's north side.


Great Falls is a cultural, commercial, and financial center in the central part of the state . It is located just east of the Rocky Mountains and is bisected by the Missouri River . The city is 180 miles from the east entrance to Glacier National Park in northern Montana and 264 miles from Yellowstone National Park in southern Montana and northern Wyoming . Great Falls makes the perfect stopover for those traveling between the two parks.


Great Falls is known for its hydroelectric dams, which give the city its moniker, “The Electric City” . The city is named after a series of five waterfalls located on the Missouri River north and east of the city . Three of the waterfalls, known as Black Eagle, Rainbow, and the Great Falls (or the Big Falls), are among the sites of five hydroelectric dams in the area.
