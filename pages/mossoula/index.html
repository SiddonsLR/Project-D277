About Missoula

Missoula is a city in western Montana, with a population of 75,516 as of the 2020 census. The city is located at the bottom of what once was Glacial Lake Missoula, a 3,000-square-mile proglacial lake which stretched from 60 miles south and east of Missoula north to today's Flathead Lake and west to Idahos Lake Pend Oreille. The history of Missoula dates back to 12,000 years ago with the end of the regions glacial lake period, with western exploration dating back to the Lewis and Clark Expedition of 1804-1806.


The city's economy is driven by several industries, including healthcare, retail, and energy. The healthcare industry is the largest employer in the city, with St. Patrick Hospital and Community Medical Center being the two largest hospitals in the region. The retail industry is also a significant contributor to the city's economy, with Southgate Mall being the largest shopping center in Montana. The energy industry is also a significant contributor to the citys economy, with the Bakken oil development and the Heath Shale oil discovery north of Missoula. The city is also home to the University of Montana, which offers undergraduate and graduate programs in various fields .


In terms of future economic direction, the city is looking to expand its technology sector and attract more high-tech companies to the area. The city is also looking to expand its tourism industry by promoting its natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities. The city is also investing in infrastructure projects, such as the Missoula International Airport expansion, which will increase the airport's capacity and improve passenger experience. The city is also investing in renewable energy projects, such as the Missoula Solar Project, which is a proposed 20-megawatt solar farm that would provide electricity to the city
