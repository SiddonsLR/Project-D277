About

Montana is a state located in the northwestern region of the United States. As of 2022, the population of Montana was estimated to be around 1,122,867 people . The state is the fourth largest in the country and is bordered by North Dakota, South Dakota, Wyoming, Idaho, and Canada. Montana is known for its diverse geography, which includes the Rocky Mountains, the Great Plains, and the Missouri River. The state is home to several national parks, including Glacier National Park and Yellowstone National Park.


Montana has a diverse economy, with industries such as agriculture, mining, and tourism playing a significant role in the state's economic growth. According to the US Census Bureau, the median household income in Montana was $60,560 in 2021, while the average household income was $82,237. The per capita income in Montana was $34,423 in 2021 . Montana's economy has been growing at a steady pace, with the state's per capita personal income growing by 8.5% in 2021, faster than the national growth rate of 7.4%.

Fun Facts

  • Montana has more cows than people. The cow population is about 2.6 million, while the human population is around 1 million
  • Montana is home to over 8,000 moose and has the largest migratory elk herd in the United States
  • Montana's Museum of the Rockies holds one of the largest dinosaur fossil collections in the world
  • Giant Springs, Montana is home to the largest freshwater spring in the U.S
  • Montana holds the world record for the greatest temperature change in 24 hours. On January 14-15, 1972, the temperature went from -54°F to 49°F, a whopping 103 degrees in Loma!
  • In Helena, Montana it is illegal to throw an item across a street
  • The Montana Yogo Sapphire is the only North American gem to be included in the Crown Jewels of England
  • In 1888 Helena had more millionaires per capita than any other city in the world
